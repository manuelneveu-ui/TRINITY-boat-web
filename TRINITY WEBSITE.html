Trinity – RC Boat Project

Trinity is a 70 cm RC monohull, designed as a two‑year engineering project at ICAM. Our goal is to reach a stable speed around 24 km/h with a reliable brushless setup and an ESP32‑based control system.

Discover the project

Trinity, when three become one

The name “Trinity” comes from the convergence of three key elements: hull, propulsion, and electronic control. When these three bricks are combined, the boat reaches its best performance.

The project is led by naval and mechanical engineering students, combining propulsion calculations, 3D design and on‑water testing.

The boat

Hull: about 70 cm monohull, optimized for stability around 24 km/h.

Propulsion: 36 mm brushless motor, marine ESC and a propeller tuned for the right balance between thrust and speed.

Electronics & control: onboard ESP32, basic sensors and a web interface that lets us monitor and adjust parameters from a smartphone.

Foiling kit: The boat is equipped with a mountable foiling kit that let the boat fly at max 20 km/h and an increase of 40% efficiency

The foil design

Profile: NACA-2412 Foils at the front and back

Form: The back foil is in T-shape and the front are "mustache" shaped

Placement: One at the back and two in the front

Flight: Start flying at 14 Km/h.Flies 6cm above the water

  • Goal – A reliable, performant boat for demos and technical presentations.
